Case study 1
Position: Inside Sales Representative
Purpose of position The Inside Sales Representative is accountable for sales orders through proactive and reactive selling and written enquires, from both a designated list of specific customers who are contacted by the Sales Representative, and non-designated accounts or prospective customers. The incumbent is also responsible for the development of technical and commercial credibility with customers.
Primary responsibilities This position is one of the six positions reporting to the Inside sales Supervisor. The other five positions are Inside Sales Representative (3) and Quotations Representatives (2).
The market is very competitive for price, mix and service. The company has a wide range of products, applications, product suppliers, customers and internal business systems. The position requires effective technical and product knowledge, communication with customers, plus monitoring and following up of the progress of quotations, orders and deliveries of services or products. The incumbent must select prices, prepare quotes, and process orders to match specific customer requirements, both technical and commercial, within business and procedures.
In addition, the incumbent must develop effective telephone selling techniques for day-to-day use with existing and/or prospective customers. The position requires effective relationships with divisions, external suppliers and internal branch functions such as order processing, credit and warehousing facilities. Technical support is available from divisions and external suppliers on technical and commercial product information and within the branch from the Product Application department, Treasury/ Credit Department, Order Process Systems, and Inventory/Warehouse facilities.
The incumbent can make sales contracts with approved customers up to $5000 without second level authority. The position provides internal back up support to 2 sales representatives through the effective handling of all customer communications and internal processing of orders, as well as contract administration of total Gross Sales Booked for the sales representatives served.
Normal work environment allows incumbent to sit comfortably and move about freely ( though generally confined to work area). There may be some need to lift light articles. The physical environment is clean and climate-controlled within a partitioned workstation. Work involves extensive periods of time on telephone, reading price lists and technical material, reviewing contracts, keyboarding, etc. deadlines are generally set by others and may be unpredictable.

Supervision Received Works under general supervision. Work is reviewed periodically to ensure conformity to established price structures, credit procedures and customer complaints.
Entrance Qualifications College in a technical field or equivalent. 2 years experience in related field with specific knowledge related to products and services that are being sold.
Specific accountabilities
1. Contributes to profit through quotation and order entry accuracy, commercial terms, price levels, and credit approval detail for designated accounts.
2. Contributes to sales through effective communications with customers to support sales Representatives' efforts.
3. Contributes to market share identification in designated accounts through record keeping and commercial information.
4. Insures effective relationships with allied support functions internally and externally ( suppliers) by developing knowledge of key personnel and resources.
5. Ensures self-development on technical and business courses within or outside the Company appropriate to the requirements of the position by attendance at adult educational institutions or company-sponsored courses.

Position:

Manufacturing Manager

Purpose of position: The Manufacturing Manager is accountable for the efficient utilization of available resources ( labour, machinery, equipment and plant capacity) so that quality manufactured components are produced on time and \Vithin operating cost targets.

Primary responsibilities

This position is one of four reporting to the plant Manager. The other 3 are Marketing Manager, Engineering Managers , and Distribution and Control Manager. Reporting to the incumbent are the following positions:

Production Supervisor; organize and supervise the factory floor to ensure that production is on time and within quality and cost standards.

\Varehouse supervisor; organize and supervise the warehouse to ensure that incoming/outgoing materials are correct and have appropriate information. Also ensures materials are stored correctly.

Material controller; provide proper progression of flow of materials, request material when required and control necessary receipts and invoice paperwork.

Production Control Clerkj complete requests for parts and materials, routine shipping documents and reports. Ensure work orders and prepare manufacturing paperwork according to established procedures.

Buyer Expenditer; purchases standard catalogue items and on occasion nonstandards items at lowest price consistent with delivery and quality requirements.

Shipping Clerk; ensures that all outgoing materials and paperwork match according to established procedures.

The incumbent is responsible for assisting the plant manager in maximizing their operating efficiencies in meeting production schedules within cost and quality standards established for the product lines. He/she is actively involved with the planning and execution of productivity improvements and development of automation projects. Once the production schedules and inventory levels have been established to meet orders, the scheduled then become final. Any change to these schedules present a major challenge. Revised scheduled may call for a reduction in production for a particular product and at the same time an increase in another product /order. Consequently, the cross training of plant personnel is important to ensure its ability to adjust to these new schedules. This is particularly challenging because concerns for excessive work in progress and inventory may increase costs resulting in an adverse impact on the plant's profitability.

He/she is responsible for ensuring that the correct material is available for when it is needed. This requires that the incumbent establish objectives per order and organize the production to meet the schedules within acceptable inventory levels.

One of the major challenges of the position is to utilize the facility in the most efficient manner. This includes the effective of the utilization of machines , labour, inventory and factory equipment. The major focus is to reduce costs, in this capacity he/she will constantly monitor and control costs that are at variance to budget. Action plans are developed by the incumbent and are implemented after the various departments have been notified.

Total quality is the responsibility of the position. He/she sets quality objectives and evaluates quality performance by reviewing warranty costs, scrap and customer feedback.

Work environment allows incumbent to move about freely. The physical environment is clean and climate controlled within a private office. Work involves reading reports, answering phones, attending meetings, etc. there is pressure to ensures production quotas, quality standards and safety objectives are being met.

Supervision received Work is assigned in terms of objectives and relative priorities. Review is periodical to ensure objectives are being met .

Entrance Qualifications Bachelor's degree in Business or equivalent. 6~ 7 years experience in a manufacturing area.

Specific Accountabilities I. Ensures efficient manufacture of products in accordance with production
schedules and within quality and costs standards. 2. Contributes to Plant profitability through prompt solution of current cost, quality,
productivity, process improvement and other manufacturing problems. 3. Develops and implements realistic short term manufacturing facilities plans and
assists with long range facilities planning. 4. Ensures present and future objectives can be met through the acquisition,
development and utilization of the plant's human, financial, equipment and material resources
