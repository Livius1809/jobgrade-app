IMPORTANT GUIDELINES WHEN EVALUATING JOBS
The following guidelines must be considered when evaluating positions. · The position's content is evaluated, not the employee. An employee is
evaluated through a performance management and salary review process. · The process is not influenced by the employee's personality, gender,
performance or seniority. The focus is only on the position requirements, regardless of who holds the position. · The process is not influenced by the supply and demand pressures that may exist in the market for certain types of skills. These issues are dealt with in other areas of the HR systems. · The position's primary areas of responsibilities are evaluated instead of infrequent or minor responsibilities. · The position's current job responsibilities are evaluated, not the future responsibilities. A separate JE Policy identifies how the process is maintained.
